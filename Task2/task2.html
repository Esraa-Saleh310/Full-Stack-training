What is Business Information Technology?
Business Information Technology is a field that sits at the intersection of two worlds: business management and information technology. The core idea is that you understand both how a business operates and how technology can be used to make that business run better. You're not purely a programmer, and you're not purely a business manager — you're the bridge between them. Think of it like a translator. Developers speak in code and technical detail; managers speak in budgets, goals, and customer needs. A BIT professional understands both languages and connects the two so the technology actually solves real business problems.

  • What you typically study
    • A BIT degree usually blends courses from both sides: Business side: management principles, accounting, finance basics, marketing, business process management, project management, and organizational behavior. Technology side: programming fundamentals, databases and SQL, networking and operating systems, web/full-stack development, systems analysis and design, and increasingly cybersecurity and data analytics. The combination is what makes the degree distinctive — you graduate able to gather business requirements, design a technical solution, and communicate it to both technical and non-technical people.
